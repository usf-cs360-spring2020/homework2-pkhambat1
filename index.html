Overview

Intergenerational Mobility in US Colleges

This webpage is for visualizing the data about Intergenerational Mobility in US Colleges and draw insights from the same by harnessing the power of data visualization. The original dataset has 85 columns and 2,203 rows of records. Hopefully the visualizations are successful in bringing non-trivial findings to the light.

Inspiration: Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility

Details

The data for the visualizations has been sourced from the Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates by College dataset from Opportunity Insights.

"(The dataset) reports our baseline estimates of parents’ and children’s income distributions by college. We calculate college-level values as means over students in the 1980, 1981 and 1982 birth cohorts. When data for a college from any of these cohorts are incomplete, we use data from 1983 and 1984 cohorts to obtain an estimate."

https://opportunityinsights.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Codebook-MRC-Table-2.pdf

The dataset displays records of each US college based on estimated children's income after graduation and estimated parental income for said student. The data is however only based on students in the cohorts between the 1980-1982 window.

Some of the more relevant columns are listed below:

  1. super_opeid, name: Institution OPEID / Cluster ID when combining multiple OPEIDs, College (or group) name
  2. type: public, private non-profit or for-profit
  3. tier, tier name: Selectivity and type combination, name for tier no.
  4. par_rank: Mean parental income rank
  5. par_q[PARQUINT]: Fraction of parents in an income quintile [PARQUINT]. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.
  6. k_rank: Mean kid earnings rank
  7. k_q[KIDQUINT]: Fraction of kids in an income quintile [KIDQUINT]. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.
  8. female: Fraction female among kids
  9. count: Average number of kids per cohort
  10. shareimputed: Share of count-weighted data that was imputed using information from the 1983-84 cohorts

Filtration

For this visualization I chose to narrow down on 7 columns. 6 of them are as follows:

Column Alias Description
par_rank Avg. Parent Rank Mean parental income rank
k_rank Avg. Student Rank Mean kid earnings rank
par_q1 % Parents Q1 Fraction of parents in an income quintile 1. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.
par_q5 % Parents Q5 Fraction of parents in an income quintile 5. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.
k_q1 % Students Q1 Fraction of kids in an income quintile 1. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.
k_q5 % Students Q5 Fraction of kids in an income quintile 5. 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top.

My decision to filter down to these was largely based on getting percentage values or rank values that have a fixed range so I can justify comparing different values against one-another.

The 7th column is a wrangled column, College Tier (group)

Wrangling

College Tier (group) is formed by classifying College Tier data into 2 main categories - Selective - Highly Selective and Less Selective / Insufficient Data. This is encoded by the color of the circles

I have excluded data that appeared to have gaps or lack of documentation. The permissible values for Share Imputed were chosen to be those between 0 and 0.5. Super OPEID values less than zero have been excluded for lack of proper information about the college.

I also excluded fields such as average cohort size and percentage of females in college since the readings did not seem to point towards any trends.
